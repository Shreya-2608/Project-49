5 Fun Fact on Camels:

1.) Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.

2.) Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.

3.) Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can’t eat.

4.) Camels can travel at up to 40 miles per hour – the same as a racehorse!

5.) Camels are very strong and can carry up to 900 pounds for 25 miles a day.

5 Fun Facts on Chameleon's:

1.) Chameleon feet work like Tongs

2.) Chameleon eyes can swivel around in 2 different directions simultaneously

3.) Smaller the Chameleon, Faster the tounge.

4.) Chameleon spit is unbelievably sticky

5.) Chameleons can't regrow their tails like other lizards.

5 Fun Facts on Pigeons:

1.) Pigeons have given company to humans for 10,000 years or more.

2.) Pigeons have saved many lifes during World War-1&2

3.) Pigeon races are very famous. It was held as a competition in 1900 Olympics Paris.

4.) Pigeons use the magnetic field to navigate

5.) Pigeon dropping and feathers are a hotbead for transmitting diseases and Bacteria

5 Fun Facts on Teddy Bears:

1.) Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.

2.) The world's smallest stitched teddy bear is a mere 0.29 inches tall! It was created by South African artist Cheryl Moss who is known for her minuscule work.

3.) Jackie Miley from Hilly City South Dakota has the world’s biggest teddy bear collection, totaling 8,026 bear friends in 2012.

4.) The first toy stuffed bear was created by German toymaker Margarete Steiff. She created a soft plush with movable arms and legs and a friendly face after taking her nephew to the zoo in 1903.

5.) The official term for a teddy bear collector is an ‘arctophile’.

5 Fun Facts on Pandas

1.) Giant pandas are good at climbing trees and can also swim.

2.) Pandas go from pink to white and black (or brown).

3.) An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day!

4.) A panda Can Poop 28 Kilos per day!

5.) The only panda in the west is Chi Chi in London.

5 Fun Facts on Flying Cicadas

1.) Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies.

2.) Most have red-orange eyes. But occasionally cicadas have blue, white or grayish eyes.

3.) Females may be attracted to the sound of motors.

4.) The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus.

5.) They improve lawns by digging tunnels that allow air into the soil. When they die, their rotting bodies put nitrogen into the soil.
